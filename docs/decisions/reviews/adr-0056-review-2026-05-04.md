## 3. Assumptions Challenged

### Assumption 3.1: Cross-service composition requires centralization (S3.1)

- **Stated Norm:** "Providers that compose multiple sibling infrastructure services MUST remain in `app/infrastructure/services/providers.py`."
- **Underlying Assumption:** Cross-service wiring distributed across packages creates untraceable dependency graphs that are hard to override for testing.
- **Challenge:** What if cross-service providers are few enough that distribution wouldn't cause confusion?
- **Evidence Strength:** ⭐ Strong
- **Counter-Evidence Found:** No — every authoritative source supports centralizing *composition* (Seemann, Cosmic Python, Fowler). The number of providers is irrelevant; the principle is that wiring logic should be in one place.
- **Confidence (ADR survives challenge):** 🟢 High
- **Reviewer Notes:** ADR-0076 S3 independently mandates this rule. S3.1 is the ADR-0056 complement. Removing S3.1 would create a contradiction with ADR-0076.

### Assumption 3.2: Self-contained providers may be distributed (S3.2)

- **Stated Norm:** "Providers that construct a single service from only their own domain settings (no sibling infrastructure dependencies) MAY be defined in their own infrastructure package."
- **Underlying Assumption:** A provider that constructs from only its own settings is not performing "composition" in the Seemann sense. Moving it to its owning module improves cohesion without fragmenting the composition graph.
- **Challenge:** Could distributed self-contained providers fragment discoverability? Could developers lose track of where providers are defined?
- **Evidence Strength:** ⭐ Strong
- **Counter-Evidence Found:** No. All six external sources place self-contained providers at their owning module. FastAPI's `get_settings()` lives in `config.py`, not in a central providers file. FastAPI Full-Stack Template's `engine` lives in `core/db.py`. Cosmic Python's adapters are defined in their own modules. Discoverability is maintained via `infrastructure.services.__init__.py` re-exports (ADR-0048 B2 consumption surface).
- **Confidence (ADR survives challenge):** 🟢 High
- **Reviewer Notes:** The "MAY" permissive posture is correct — it does not *require* distribution, only *permits* it. Providers may remain in `providers.py` if the team prefers. This avoids a disruptive mandate while opening the door for improvement.

### Assumption 3.3: ADR-0048 B2 governs consumption surface, not definition location

- **Stated Norm:** "ADR-0048 B2 governs the consumption surface (where features import from), not the definition location (where provider functions are written)."
- **Underlying Assumption:** B2's "single injection boundary" means features import from `infrastructure.services`, not that providers must be defined in a single file.
- **Challenge:** Could B2 have been intended to mandate definition-location centralization?
- **Evidence Strength:** ⭐ Strong
- **Counter-Evidence Found:** No. B2 reads: "Providers must be consumed through a single injection boundary." The word "consumed" explicitly describes the import-side contract, not the definition-side. Python re-exports (`__init__.py`) are the standard mechanism for maintaining a unified consumption surface across distributed definitions.
- **Confidence (ADR survives challenge):** 🟢 High
- **Reviewer Notes:** This correction is the most important part of the amendment. The original ADR-0056 S3 used B2 to justify blanket centralization — a misapplication that conflated "where features import from" with "where providers are written." The amendment corrects this by separating the two concerns explicitly.

### Assumption 3.4: Factory functions remain internal implementation details (S3.3)

- **Stated Norm:** "Infrastructure subsystems may define module-level factory functions as internal implementation details."
- **Underlying Assumption:** Factory functions like `build_google_directory_provider()` are internal to the infrastructure package and called by the composition root provider.
- **Challenge:** Could this create confusion about what is a "provider" vs a "factory"?
- **Evidence Strength:** ⭐ Strong
- **Counter-Evidence Found:** No. S3.3 was not changed by the amendment — it existed in the original ADR. The distinction is clear: providers are `@lru_cache` singletons in the composition root or owning package; factory functions are helper functions called by providers.
- **Confidence (ADR survives challenge):** 🟢 High
- **Reviewer Notes:** No change from prior review. S3.3 is stable.

## 7. Tradeoffs Accepted

### Tradeoff 7.1: Permissive ("MAY") vs Mandatory ("MUST distribute") for self-contained providers

- **Chosen:** S3.2 uses "MAY" — self-contained providers *may* move to their own package but are not required to.
- **Rejected:** Mandating all self-contained providers move out of `providers.py` immediately.
- **Rationale:** Permissive posture avoids a disruptive mass migration. The team can move providers incrementally as they touch the relevant code. The key correction is removing the *prohibition* on distribution, not mandating it.
- **Risk Accepted:** `providers.py` may remain a large file if the team does not exercise the "MAY" permission. This is acceptable because the file works today and will naturally shrink as services are touched for other reasons (ADR-0055 dissolution, ADR-0077 Protocol migration).
- **Contingency:** If `providers.py` exceeds 25 cached providers (the threshold noted in the Consequences section), reassess whether to strengthen "MAY" to "SHOULD".

### Tradeoff 7.2: Correctness vs Stability of cross-references

- **Chosen:** Correct the B2 misapplication in ADR-0056; accept that downstream ADR cross-references (ADR-0076 Compliance) use the old title.
- **Rejected:** Amending every downstream reference simultaneously.
- **Rationale:** The normative content of ADR-0076 S3 is correct and unaffected. The Compliance section's phrasing is editorial, not normative.
- **Risk Accepted:** A reader of ADR-0076 may see "centralized `providers.py`" and think S3.2 doesn't exist. Low probability — ADR-0056 S3 is the authoritative record.
- **Contingency:** Editorial update to ADR-0076 Compliance section (non-blocking follow-up).
